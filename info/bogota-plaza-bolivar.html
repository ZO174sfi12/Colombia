🏛️ Plaza de Bolívar

Bogotá · Colombia

De Plaza de Bolívar is het historische en symbolische hart van Bogotá en heel Colombia. Aan dit plein — omgeven door imposante gebouwen die vijf eeuwen geschiedenis vertegenwoordigen — staan de Catedral Primada, het Capitolio Nacional, het Palacio de Justicia en het Liévano-paleis (stadhuis).

In het midden staat het beroemde bronzen standbeeld van Simón Bolívar, de bevrijder van Colombia. Het plein is een ontmoetingsplek voor Bogotanen en toeristen, wordt gebruikt voor nationale ceremonies en laat- zie je er soms Colombianen die je fotograferen met hun llama voor een paar pesos.

📗 Lonely Planet Colombia 2023

Get orientated in the Plaza de Bolívar where the city is thought to have been founded in 1538. You’ll be naturally drawn to the neoclassical facade of the gigantic Catedral Primada, but also reserve time for the Capitolio Nacional and the Casa de Nariño that sits behind it. Afternoon

Grab lunch in La Puerta Falsa and stroll up La Séptima, spending the best part of the afternoon in the extraordinary Museo del Oro. Evening

Pass the evening admiring creative street art and dipping into the bars around the Plazoleta del Chorro de Quevedo. Be sure to decamp to Origen Bistro for dinner.

Get up with the larks and hike to the top of Cerro de Monserrate for an early-morning fitness test. Back down at the base, pay a visit to the Casa Museo Quinta de Bolívar before grabbing a deli sandwich at Chantonner Delikatessen. Afternoon

Work your way north stopping at the Museo Nacional and the Torre Colpatria. Consider a side trip to the San Felipe Art District to meander between galleries, bars and offbeat street sculpture. Evening

Make a dinner date at a restaurant in Zona G and fill what’s left of the evening bar-hopping in the Zona Rosa.

Start in the historic grid of La Candelaria absorbing the life and flavors of modern-day Bogotá from street level. Endeavor to devote at least an hour or two to the seminal Museo Botero. Afternoon

Admire the trilogy of churches along La Séptima and then catch a TransMilenio bus to Chapinero. Sample a flight of coffee tasters at Azahar Café and explore the shops and greenery of Parque de la 93 before investigating the Museo El Chicó. Evening

Call a taxi to take you out to the party emporium better known as Andrés Carne de Res. You’ll Also Want To… When you’ve covered all the obvious sights, veer off-piste on a hike or bike-ride, seeking out esoteric street snacks and powerful coffee. Partake IN Ciclovia Bogotá’s Ciclovia is one of the world’s oldest and largest car-free Sundays. You won’t know Bogotá until you’ve taken part.. GO Looking For Emeralds Colombia is the world’s leading emerald producer and Bogotá’s Emerald Trade Center is a good place to ponder a purchase.

📘 Rough Guide to Colombia 2024

Plaza de Bolívar Explore Bogotá’s history via its cathedral, law courts and a couple of palaces – or have your photo taken with a llama. Museo Botero Marvel at the biggest and best collection of works by Colombia’s favourite artist, all for free. Museo del Oro (Gold Museum) Stuffed to the rafters with gold, gold and more gold from pre-Hispanic Colombia – no wonder the Spanish thought it was El Dorado. Cerro de Monserrate Take the funicular or a cable car, or just climb up the stairs to this popular pilgrimage site for a fantastic bird’s-eye view over the whole city. Cementerio Central Wander this fascinating cemetery where most of Colombia’s presidents lie buried, as well as British and Irish troops who fell fighting for Bolívar. Dine out in La Macarena This bohemian inner-city neighbourhood has become Bogotá’s favourite place to eat out, full of new and innovative restaurants serving cuisine from Colombia, South America and the world. Zona Rosa nightlife The epicentre of Bogotá’s evening entertainment is chock-full of bars, clubs, cafés and restaurants, and throbs to the beat of everything from house or hip-hop to rock or salsa. 💡 Tips

• Bezoek de Catedral Primada — vrij toegankelijk, indrukwekkend interieur

• Het Capitolio Nacional biedt soms rondleidingen op werkdagen

• Het Palacio de Justicia is herbouwd na de dramatische bestorming van 1985

• Mooi fotomoment: vroeg in de ochtend als het plein nog leeg is

• Combineer met Museo del Oro (5 min lopen) en Iglesia de San Francisco
