🚡 Cerro de Monserrate

Bogotá · Colombia

De Cerro de Monserrate is de iconische berg die Bogotá domineert — het kerkje op de top is van bijna overal in de stad te zien. Op 3152 m hoogte biedt hij een oogverblindend panorama over de uitgestrekte metropool. Je bereikt de top per kabelbaan, funiculaire trein of te voet via een steile pelgrimsroute.

Bovenaan tref je de Basílica del Señor Caído, meerdere restaurants en terrasjes. De berg is ook een populaire pelgrimsbestemming — elke zondag trekken duizenden Bogotanen naar boven voor de mis. Plan je bezoek bij voorkeur 's ochtends voor minder drukte en minder kans op bewolking.

📗 Lonely Planet Colombia 2023

The sentinel Eastern Hills, dominated by the church-topped Cerro de Monserrate, provide a welcome green backdrop. The city’s grittier sides sit south and southwest of La Candelaria, where working-class barrios, not yet tourist magnets, have shrugged off the no-go reputations of yesteryear. Skyscrapers crowd the Centro Internacional’s business district, while streets to the north have boutique hotels and fashionably clad locals perusing chic entertainment in the Zona Rosa. Here, rust-tinted sunsets bounce off the terracotta bricks of Bogotá’s Andes-hugging residential towers – a cinematic ceremony that ushers in the city’s uproarious evenings. La Candelaria

Get orientated in the Plaza de Bolívar where the city is thought to have been founded in 1538. You’ll be naturally drawn to the neoclassical facade of the gigantic Catedral Primada, but also reserve time for the Capitolio Nacional and the Casa de Nariño that sits behind it. Afternoon

Grab lunch in La Puerta Falsa and stroll up La Séptima, spending the best part of the afternoon in the extraordinary Museo del Oro. Evening

Pass the evening admiring creative street art and dipping into the bars around the Plazoleta del Chorro de Quevedo. Be sure to decamp to Origen Bistro for dinner.

Get up with the larks and hike to the top of Cerro de Monserrate for an early-morning fitness test. Back down at the base, pay a visit to the Casa Museo Quinta de Bolívar before grabbing a deli sandwich at Chantonner Delikatessen. Afternoon

Work your way north stopping at the Museo Nacional and the Torre Colpatria.

📘 Rough Guide to Colombia 2024

Cerro de Monserrate Take the funicular or a cable car, or just climb up the stairs to this popular pilgrimage site for a fantastic bird’s-eye view over the whole city. Cementerio Central Wander this fascinating cemetery where most of Colombia’s presidents lie buried, as well as British and Irish troops who fell fighting for Bolívar. Dine out in La Macarena This bohemian inner-city neighbourhood has become Bogotá’s favourite place to eat out, full of new and innovative restaurants serving cuisine from Colombia, South America and the world. Zona Rosa nightlife The epicentre of Bogotá’s evening entertainment is chock-full of bars, clubs, cafés and restaurants, and throbs to the beat of everything from house or hip-hop to rock or salsa. 💡 Tips

• Ga voor 10u — minder rijen en de kans op wolken is kleiner

• Neem een warme laag mee — bovenaan is het significant frisser

• Op weekdagen veel rustiger dan weekends

• Te voet kan ook (gratis) maar is steil en fysiek zwaar — niet aanbevolen op dag 2 na aankomst

• Bovenkant: de Señor Caído kerk, meerdere restaurants en fototerras met panoramisch uitzicht
