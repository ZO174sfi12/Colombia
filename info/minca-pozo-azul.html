💧 Pozo Azul

Minca · Colombia

Pozo Azul is een kristalhelder bergbeekje net buiten Minca — een reeks kleine watervallen en zwemplekken met stralend azuurblauw-turquoise water. De naam ('blauwe put') zegt het al: het water heeft een onwerkelijke kleur door de mineraalsamenstelling en de weerkaatsing van het zonlicht.

Het is een ideale plek om af te koelen na een wandeling of motorritje door het dorp. De tochtig rit erheen — vaak per motortaxi — door weelderig jungleland is al een beleving. Pozo Azul is een van de eerste activiteiten die toeristen doen na aankomst in Minca.

Swimming Pozo Azul and the Cascadas de Marinka are two popular natural swimming pools. The latter has a small entrance fee, but fewer crowds, a cafe and giant nets for lounging. Bird-watching Jungle Joe Minca takes groups on three-hour birding tours daily at 6am. Alternatively, birding haven Reserva Natural El Dorado has sweeping views from the San Lorenzo ridge. ’S Dramatic Natural Beauty The Parque Nacional Natural Tayrona, one of Colombia’s most-visited national parks, takes up an immense chunk of the Caribbean coastline and is characterized by giant boulders bookending palm-fringed inlets with golden sand tumbling under the waves. The park is 30 to 45 minutes east of Santa Marta by bus from the Mercado Público. Don’t expect instant gratification at the park entrances El Zaino or Calabazo ; it takes two to three hours hiking along ancient Indigenous footpaths, past the Pueblito ruins (currently closed to visitors), to reach the most scenic spots, such as Cabo San Juan de la Guia with its thatched-roof structure perched high on a rocky outcrop called El Mirador. Day-trippers barely get a chance to dip a toe in; a night or two is needed to feel immersed. The cheapest way is to bring a tent and stay in a designated camping area for a small fee. You can also travel light and spend an unforgettable night swinging in one of the 16 hammocks in El Mirador’s hut, or stay in an ecolodge at playas Brava, Arrecife or Cañaveral. Bring cash to cover the park entrance, mandatory insurance for emergencies (separate to personal travel insurance), shuttle from El Zaino to the trailhead, lodging, food and drinks. A fresh coconut at Punta Las Gaviotas or the famous chocolate bread at Panadería Bere are well-earned treats. Cabo San Juan de la Guia
